# Mushroom Risotto

## Ingredients (Serves 4–5)

### Mushrooms
- 500 g mushrooms, sliced
- 250 g mushrooms, quartered
- 3 tbsp butter
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper

### Risotto
- 1 tbsp butter
- 1 garlic clove, minced
- 2 eschallots (or 1/2 onion), finely chopped
- 1/4 cup dry white wine
- 1 1/4 cups arborio rice
- 5 cups chicken stock, kept warm

### Finish
- 1/2 cup cream (optional)
- 2 tbsp butter (optional)
- 1/2 cup parmesan, finely grated
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- Parsley or chives, finely chopped (to serve)

---

## Method

### 1. Cook the Mushrooms
1. Heat 1 tbsp butter and 1 tbsp olive oil in a large heavy pot over high heat.
2. Add half the mushrooms and cook for about 4 minutes until golden.
3. Add 1 minced garlic clove, 1/4 tsp salt, and 1/4 tsp pepper. Cook for 1–2 minutes.
4. Remove to a bowl.
5. Repeat with remaining mushrooms using another 1 tbsp butter and 1 tbsp olive oil. Set aside.

### 2. Start the Risotto
1. Reduce heat to medium. Add 1 tbsp butter.
2. Add 1 minced garlic clove and 2 chopped eschallots. Cook for 2 minutes until soft.
3. Add 1/4 cup white wine and simmer until mostly evaporated.
4. Stir in 1 1/4 cups arborio rice and cook for 1 minute until edges look slightly translucent.

### 3. Add Stock Gradually
1. Add about 1 1/2 cups warm chicken stock. Stir once or twice and let it absorb.
2. When mostly absorbed, add another 1 1/2 cups stock.
3. Repeat with another 1 1/2 cups stock.
4. Add the remaining stock as needed, stirring occasionally, until rice is tender but slightly firm in the centre and the mixture is creamy (about 15–20 minutes total).

### 4. Finish
1. Stir in 1/2 cup cream and 2 tbsp butter (if using).
2. Add 1/2 cup grated parmesan, 1/2 tsp salt, and 1/2 tsp pepper. Stir well until creamy.
3. Fold in half the cooked mushrooms.

### 5. Serve
1. Spoon into bowls immediately.
2. Top with remaining mushrooms, extra parmesan, and chopped parsley or chives.
