## üë©‚Äçüç≥ Instructions

### 1. Prepare the Seitan  
- In a bowl, toss **400 g seitan** with **2 tbsp cornstarch**, **1 tsp turmeric**, **¬Ω tsp salt**, and **1 tbsp oil** until evenly coated.  
- Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat.  
- Pan-fry the coated seitan for **4‚Äì5 minutes**, turning occasionally, until golden and slightly crisp.  
- Remove from pan and set aside.

---

### 2. Build the Sauce  
- In the same pan, melt **2 tbsp butter** over medium heat.  
- Add **1 finely chopped onion** and cook for **5 minutes**, stirring until soft and translucent.  
- Add **3 minced garlic cloves** and **1 tbsp grated ginger**, and cook for **1 minute** until aromatic.  
- Sprinkle in **1 tsp garam masala**, **1 tsp cumin**, **1 tsp coriander**, **¬Ω tsp paprika**, and **¬º tsp chili powder**.  
- Stir for **30 seconds** to toast the spices.  
- Add **400 g tomato passata**, stirring well to deglaze the pan.  
- Simmer gently for **5 minutes** to reduce slightly.

---

### 3. Finish the Curry  
- Stir in **200 ml double cream**, **¬Ω tsp salt**, and a dash of **lemon juice**.  
- Return the browned seitan to the pan and stir to coat evenly.  
- Simmer for **8‚Äì10 minutes** on low heat until the sauce thickens and the seitan absorbs the flavours.  
- Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt, lemon, or butter as needed.

---

### 4. Serve  
- Spoon over **basmati rice**, or serve with **naan** or **chapati**.  
- Garnish with **fresh coriander**, **toasted nuts**, and a **drizzle of cream or butter**.  

> The texture should be smooth and velvety, with a rich tomato-butter aroma and just a hint of spice.
