# Butternut Pasta with Sage & Hazelnuts (Serves 4)

A cosy, autumn-style pasta with soft squash, fragrant sage, and toasted hazelnuts.

## Ingredients
- 2 tbsp (≈30 ml) olive oil  
- 1 large onion, finely chopped  
- 1 kg butternut squash (or pumpkin), peeled and cut into cubes  
- 2 cloves garlic, finely sliced  
- Small handful of sage leaves (about 10–12 leaves), roughly chopped  
- 500 ml vegetable stock  
- 500 g short pasta (fusilli, penne, rigatoni, etc.)  
- 50–70 g hazelnuts, toasted and roughly chopped  
- Salt & pepper  
- Grated parmesan or pecorino (optional)

---

## Instructions

### 1. Cook the vegetables
1. Heat **2 tbsp (≈30 ml) olive oil** in a large pot over medium heat.  
2. Add **1 chopped onion** and cook for about **10 minutes**, until soft but not browned.  
3. Add **1 kg cubed butternut squash**, **2 sliced garlic cloves**, and **a handful of chopped sage leaves**.  
4. Stir for 1 minute, then pour in **500 ml vegetable stock**.  
5. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, cover, and simmer for **15–20 minutes**, until the squash is tender.

---

### 2. Cook the pasta
1. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil.  
2. Add **500 g pasta** and cook until **al dente** according to the packet.  
3. Reserve **250 ml (1 cup)** of the pasta cooking water, then drain.

---

### 3. Make the sauce
1. Once the squash is tender, mash it gently with a spoon or potato masher to create a sauce—creamy but still slightly chunky.  
2. If it looks too thick, add **50–100 ml** of the reserved pasta water to loosen it.

---

### 4. Combine everything
1. Add the drained **500 g cooked pasta** to the squash sauce.  
2. Mix until evenly coated, adding more pasta water as needed for creaminess.  
3. Season with **salt and pepper** to taste.  
4. Stir in **most of the 50–70 g chopped hazelnuts**, keeping some aside for serving.

---

### 5. Serve
Top with:
- Remaining **hazelnuts**  
- Extra **sage leaves** (fresh or lightly fried in a teaspoon of olive oil)  
- **Grated parmesan/pecorino**, if you like
